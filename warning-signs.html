Warning Signs: When to be Concerned

Overview

Differences between concening behavior and normal development, reassurance that there is help for all students (3-4 sentences)

Pre-K

ADHD

Inability to sit still for calm activities such as eating or having a book read to them; running from toy to toy; displaying extreme impatience with others. (Source: CDC.gov)

Anxiety

Worry may be evidenced by separation anxiety and refusal to attend school or camp or to demand that someone stay with them at bedtime or selective mutism, which is a refusal to speak. (Source: adda.gov)

Depression

Frequent crying, irritable mood, mood swings. (Source: adda.gov)

These symptoms may occur within any age range.

Elementary

ADHD

Inability to focus on one activity; trouble completing task before getting bored; difficulty following directions that require planning or executing a plan. (Source: CDC.gov)

Anxiety

Excessive worry about a variety of things such as grades, family issues, relationships with peers, and performance in sports, may include obsessive-compulsive behaviors, social anxiety and/or panic attacks. (Source: adda.gov)

Depression

Change in grades, getting into trouble at school, or refusing to go to school, low self-esteem, feeling angry, depressed or irritable. (Source: adda.gov)

These symptoms may occur within any age range.

Developmental/Learning Disorders

Difficulty remembering what was just said or just read; Difficulty understanding and following directions or staying organized; Difficulty with reading, writing and/or mathematics

Secondary

ADHD

Struggle in meeting expectations for turning assignments in on time and/or being organized; Does not comprehend the consequences of risk-taking behavior. (Source: CDC.gov)

Anxiety

Excessive worry about a variety of things such as grades, family issues, relationships with peers, and performance in sports, may include obsessive-compulsive behaviors, social anxiety and/or panic attacks. (Source: adda.gov)

Depression

Withdrawal, change in eating or sleeping habits, loss of energy, thoughts of death or suicide. (Source: adda.gov)

These symptoms may occur within any age range.

Developmental/Learning Disorders

Difficulty remembering what was just said or just read; Difficulty understanding and following directions or staying organized; Difficulty with reading, writing and/or mathematics
